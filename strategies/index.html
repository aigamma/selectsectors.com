The strategy library.

Six strategies, all implemented in crates/backtest-core/src/strategies/, all dispatched through the StrategyKind enum, all subject to the no-lookahead constraint the engine enforces. Each page below pairs the intuition and math with the actual Rust source that the WASM engine runs on every backtest.

Catalog

  1. 01 Buy and hold Always long, no parameters. The reference benchmark every other strategy is judged against.
  2. 02 SMA crossover Long when the fast simple moving average is above the slow simple moving average. The textbook trend- following signal.
  3. 03 Momentum Long when today's close exceeds the close lookback bars ago. The oldest documented factor in modern finance.
  4. 04 RSI mean reversion Enter long when Wilder's RSI dips below oversold; exit to flat above overbought. A fade-the-dip strategy.
  5. 05 Donchian breakout Long when today's close meets or exceeds the rolling high of the prior lookback bars. The Donchian / Turtle Traders rule.
  6. 06 Bollinger Bands mean reversion Enter long when the close drops below SMA − k × std; exit at the SMA centerline. A dispersion- based fade-the-dip, complementary to RSI.

The "Try it" buttons

Every strategy page has a Try it link that jumps to the homepage with the strategy and sensible defaults pre-filled, so you can run a backtest on it in two clicks. Pick a symbol on the form, hit Run, and the result lands in the result panel with metrics and an equity chart.

Or compare all six at once: /compare/ runs every strategy in the catalog against the same symbol and date range in a single rate-limit slot, with the equity curves overlaid and the metrics ranked by Sharpe.
